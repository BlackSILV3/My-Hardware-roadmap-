STAGE 05D ✦ CRITICAL
Tcl Scripting for EDA
Tcl (Tool Command Language) is the embedded scripting interface in virtually every commercial EDA tool. Synopsys Design Compiler, PrimeTime, IC Compiler, Cadence Innovus, Tempus, Xilinx Vivado, Intel Quartus -- all use Tcl as their primary command language.
⚠ Critical Gap Closed -- Industry's Hidden Lingua Franca

Why this is critical: Every synthesis script, every STA report generation, every floorplan automation, every regression testbench invocation in industry is driven by Tcl. A VLSI engineer who cannot write Tcl cannot automate their workflow, cannot customize tool behavior, and cannot extract and analyze the massive reports these tools generate. Python (Stage 5C) is valuable but cannot replace Tcl for direct EDA tool control -- they serve different layers of the workflow.

STAGE 06 + SVA
Digital Logic & Computer Design + SystemVerilog Assertions
Logic gates to Boolean algebra to FSMs to datapaths to control units to processor. Now enhanced with SystemVerilog Assertions (SVA) -- the formal property checking language used to catch bugs before simulation. Ben Eater makes you watch it happen physically on breadboard.
Currently Active With Instructor -- SVA Gap Closed

Formal Verification Gap Closed: SystemVerilog Assertions (SVA) are used to formally prove that a design meets its specification. Unlike simulation (which tests specific inputs), formal verification mathematically explores all possible inputs. This is one of the fastest-growing specializations in chip design -- companies have dedicated formal verification teams because catching bugs before tape-out saves millions in respin costs.

The critical bridge between analog and digital. ADCs, DACs, PLLs, clock domains, and the challenges of integrating analog blocks into digital SoCs. Modern chips are overwhelmingly mixed-signal -- separating analog from digital is an academic luxury, not an industry reality.

STAGE 11B ✦ CRITICAL

⚠ Critical Gaps Closed: STA + Low Power + UVM
